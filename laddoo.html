Why I'm doing this

Getting a cat turned me into a market researcher.

When Laddoo came home, I started researching what to feed her — and kept running into the same three problems everywhere I looked.

The store shelf

Walk into Heads Up For Tails or most pet stores in India: 80–90% of the floor is dog products. Cat parents get a shelf or two. Retail hasn't caught up with where pet ownership is actually growing.

The information mess

There's no reliable answer to "what should my cat eat?" Advice is scattered and contradictory, mass-market labels are hard to trust ("meat derivatives", BHA, starch fillers), and even I — after months of research — still have open questions about what a complete meal needs.

The current fix isn't one

New-age brands solve the preservative problem but cost ~₹84 per meal — out of reach for most Indian households. And it's still packaged food at the end of the day. Cats do best on fresh food, and nobody is really solving for fresh.

Don't take my word for it

This is what cat parents in India sound like right now.

Real threads from r/IndianCats and r/IndianPets. Confusion, contradiction, budget stress, and broken trust — in the parents' own words.

Five people, five different answers — and a cat parent stuck in the middle saying "I can't afford something much expensive."
"Best brands worldwide" → "So confidently wrong" → "Royal Canin is crap, they use caramel." Even confident advice cancels itself out.
Worms found in Whiskas kitten food — from a rescuer who trusted the brand because even vets recommend it. Trust, once broken, moves the whole market.

This isn't a niche complaint — it's the everyday experience of a growing customer base with money to spend and nowhere reliable to spend it.

The gaps

"Preservative-free" got solved. Everything else didn't.

Freshness

Everything is still packaged

Even the cleanest new-age brands sell shelf-stable pouches. Cats do best on fresh food — and no one in India has built a fresh-first model, like weekly-prepared food delivered on a schedule. This is the biggest open lane in the category.

Price

Clean food is priced like a luxury

At ~₹72–90 per pouch, feeding a cat clean food costs ₹4,000–5,000/month — untenable for most Indian households. The affordable-and-honest middle between Whiskas and the premium tier is wide open.

Reach

New-age brands can't actually reach you

Zoomies and peers are D2C-first with thin distribution. Local pet stores, vets, and neighbourhood retail — where most first purchases happen — are barely integrated.

Everything else

Treats and cat products are a trust lottery

Nobody is seriously building clean, affordable cat treats in India. And beyond food — harnesses, carriers, litter — it's unbranded imports on Amazon with no one to vouch for them. Cat parents don't need more options; they need one brand they can trust.

The plan

What I want to build

Fresh food, delivered on a schedule

Freshly prepared cat food on a weekly delivery model — real meat, complete nutrition, no shelf-stable compromise. The thing no one in India is doing, and the thing cats actually need.

Wet and dry food at an honest price

Named real meat, no BHA, no vague "derivatives" — priced for the middle, between Whiskas and the ₹84-a-meal tier, with verifiable ingredients on every pack.

Treats

Clean, India-made treats — a category no one is building here. Low-cost first purchase, high frequency, quick-commerce friendly, and the easiest way to earn trust before a food switch.

Distribution that includes local stores and vets

Not D2C-only. Integrate the neighbourhood pet stores and clinics where most first purchases actually happen.

Eventually: the cat brand you can default to

Curated essentials beyond food — litter, harnesses, carriers that actually work — so cat parents have one trusted answer instead of a lottery.

The customer is always right. Especially this one.
