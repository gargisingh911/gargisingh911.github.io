01

EN launching logic — which vendor, for which project, and why

Vendor strategy

Cost optimisation

Currently building

THE PROBLEM

Bridgetown works with multiple expert networks — NewtonX, Dialectica, Ace, and others. For every project, the wrong vendor means higher cost, slower turnaround, or lower quality experts. No structured logic existed for which EN to launch with.

WHAT I'M BUILDING

A vendor selection framework that decides which expert networks to engage based on project characteristics: client profile, nature of research, TAT requirements, panel type, and historical success rates per EN. Strategy, not guesswork.

WHY IT MATTERS

This is a cost and quality optimisation engine. Each EN has a negotiated CPI (cost per interview). I own both the CPI negotiations and the logic that deploys those vendors — directly affecting margin and client satisfaction on every single project.

02

Client pre-approval workflow — a product feature I proposed and shipped

Product strategy

Client experience

End to end

THE PROBLEM

Clients were apprehensive about who would show up for their research interviews. No visibility before the call. This caused friction, distrust, and drop-offs — a client experience problem disguised as an ops problem.

WHAT I PROPOSED

I identified this as a product gap, not a comms fix. Proposed a pre-approval workflow where clients review and approve experts before confirmation. Took it from idea to design to shipped feature — fully end to end.

THE RESULT

A live, client-facing workflow that reduced apprehension, improved trust, and strengthened client relationships — without adding significant ops overhead. I owned the why, the what, and the how.

03

Autopublish — making quality decisions without humans in the loop

Fraud detection

Quality systems

Shipped & live

THE PROBLEM

Every transcript from every expert call needed manual review before publishing to clients. Slow, inconsistent, unscalable. The team was the bottleneck. Volume was growing.

WHAT I DESIGNED

Defined the three signals that determine whether a transcript should publish — then drove the build of an automated system around them. Not a workflow change. A product decision: what does "good" look like, encoded into a system?

LIVE IN PRODUCTION

Three automated checks now run on every transcript. Only edge cases reach a human reviewer. The quality function scaled without scaling headcount — that's the only kind of system worth building.

!

Fraud detection

Automatically flags LLM or bot-generated responses. Catches AI-generated expert answers before they reach clients.

★

Quality score /5

Every transcript rated out of 5. Consistent, objective publish decisions without a human in the loop every time.

✓

LinkedIn identity match

Cross-references expert identity. Flags mismatches for human review. Protects client data integrity end to end.

04

Ops dashboard — 100+ projects, one command centre

Operations design

Product thinking

Team enablement

THE PROBLEM

100+ active expert recruitment projects across multiple clients with no central tracking. Status lived in people's heads. No visibility on what was blocked, active, or overdue. No way to assign or monitor ownership at scale.

WHAT I SHAPED

Defined what the dashboard needed to show and why. Two core views: Requests (project pipeline, status, EN success rates, owner assignment) and Review (transcript compliance, fraud/quality signals, network performance). Strategic input that turned a tool into a decision engine.

HOW IT RUNS TODAY

My team of 4 uses it daily to manage workload and assignments. Leadership uses it for resource decisions. Every vendor success rate, every project status, every transcript in queue — one place. No more chasing updates.

05

Expert network expansion — filling sourcing gaps strategically

Procurement

Strategic partnerships

Supply strategy

THE PROBLEM

Primary research sourcing had coverage gaps — certain expert profiles, geographies, or industries were hard to fill through existing ENs. Pushing existing vendors harder wasn't the answer.

WHAT I DID

Identified where the gaps were. Evaluated new expert networks, led outreach, negotiated CPIs, and onboarded them. Then fed that data into the EN launching logic — so the right EN gets deployed per project, not just the familiar one.

THE RESULT

A more diverse, cost-optimised vendor ecosystem. EN success rates are tracked in the dashboard per project type. Vendor decisions are now data-driven — not relationship-driven.
