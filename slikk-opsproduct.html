The real problem — and it's not what most people say
The return problem is a symptom. The disease is pre-dispatch quality.
Everyone talks about Slikk's return experience being slow. But look at the 1-star reviews — the pattern is specific: products arriving worn, missing tags, smelling used, visibly defective. Customers aren't returning because they changed their mind. They're returning because the item that arrived wasn't what was promised. That's not a returns ops problem. That's a pre-dispatch quality control failure. Fix the quality gate before dispatch, and the return volume drops dramatically — making the returns experience easier to fix too. One problem upstream eliminates two problems downstream.

What Slikk already nails
The delivery promise is real
60-min delivery, curated selection, Try & Buy, clean app. The core product works. That's rare for a 2-year-old startup. The $10M Series A from Nexus + Lightspeed confirms the market believes it too.
The gap I'd fix first
Pre-dispatch quality gate — before the item leaves the dark store
A structured quality check at dispatch: tag present, condition verified, no visible defects. Log it. If something slips through, the customer has photographic proof at origin — making return disputes instant to resolve. This is exactly the kind of system I've built.
