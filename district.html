A gap I've been thinking about
"People want to meet strangers over great food. Nobody has made it feel natural yet."
The supper club format has existed forever. But in India it still feels either too expensive, too curated, or too try-hard. The organic version — the one that feels like stumbling into someone's dinner party — doesn't exist at scale yet. That's the product District could build. Not a restaurant. A recurring social format.
The problem
Social dining is either too expensive or too forced
Supper clubs exist. But the price point excludes most of the people who'd most love it. And the ones that are affordable feel like networking events with better food. Nobody has cracked the version that feels genuinely organic.
The insight
The meal is the excuse. The people are the product.
What people actually want isn't a great restaurant. It's a great story about a Tuesday night they didn't plan for. The food is just the reason to be in the same room. District could own this format before anyone else names it.
